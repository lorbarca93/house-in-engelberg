This sensitivity analysis examines how key variables impact the financial performance of the Engelberg property investment. Each scenario tests different assumptions to understand the range of possible outcomes and identify the most critical factors affecting cash flow and returns.

Base Case Parameters: Purchase price CHF 1,300,000 CHF, 4 owners, 63% occupancy, CHF 200 average daily rate. Base case annual cash flow: -19,262 CHF.

Sensitivity Impact Analysis

The following visualizations provide a comprehensive overview of how different sensitivity factors impact the investment. The first chart shows annual cash-on-cash (unlevered) efficiency - how much money you need to put in or can make per year. Subsequent tornado charts show NPV and IRR impacts, and waterfall charts illustrate cumulative impacts across all sensitivity factors.

Monthly Cash Flow per Investor Sensitivity Analysis

This chart shows the monthly cash flow impact for each investor in the property. This is the most practical metric, showing how much money each investor will need to pay monthly (negative values, left side in red) or will receive monthly (positive values, right side in green) under different scenarios. The monthly cash flow is calculated as the annual cash flow per investor divided by 12 months.

Red bars (left) show worst-case scenarios where investors need to contribute money monthly to keep the investment running. Green bars (right) show best-case scenarios where investors receive monthly cash distributions.

Hover over each bar to see the exact parameter values tested (minimum and maximum extremes), the monthly cash flow amounts, and the annual equivalent.

Cap Rate Sensitivity Analysis (Unlevered)

This chart shows the impact of each sensitivity factor on the Cap Rate (Capitalization Rate), which is calculated as Net Operating Income (NOI) divided by Purchase Price. Cap Rate is an unlevered metric that measures the property's operating performance independent of financing structure. It represents the unlevered yield on the property investment. Higher cap rates indicate better operating performance. Red bars (left) show scenarios that reduce the cap rate, green bars (right) show scenarios that increase it.

Hover over each bar to see the exact parameter values tested (minimum and maximum extremes), the cap rate impact in percentage points, and the resulting cap rate values.

Cash-on-Cash Return Sensitivity Analysis (Levered)

This chart shows the impact of each sensitivity factor on the Cash-on-Cash Return, which is calculated as (Cash Flow After Debt Service / Initial Equity Investment) 100. Cash-on-Cash Return is a levered metric that measures the annual return on the equity investment after debt service. It shows how much cash return you receive on your invested equity. Higher returns indicate better investment performance. Red bars (left) show scenarios that reduce the return, green bars (right) show scenarios that increase it.

Hover over each bar to see the exact parameter values tested (minimum and maximum extremes) and the range covered by each sensitivity analysis.

NPV Impact Analysis

This tornado chart shows how each sensitivity factor impacts the Net Present Value (NPV) of the investment over 15 years. NPV represents the total discounted value of all future cash flows. Red bars (left) show negative impact (reduces NPV), green bars (right) show positive impact (increases NPV). The longer the bar, the greater the impact.

Hover over each bar to see the exact parameter values tested (minimum and maximum extremes) and the range covered by each sensitivity analysis. Each sensitivity shows both upper and lower extremes.

IRR Impact Analysis

This tornado chart shows how each sensitivity factor impacts the Internal Rate of Return (IRR) of the investment. IRR is the annualized return percentage, including both operating cash flows and the property sale at the end of 15 years. Higher IRR means better returns. Red bars (left) show scenarios that reduce IRR, green bars (right) show scenarios that increase IRR.

Hover over each bar to see the exact parameter values tested (minimum and maximum extremes) and the range covered by each sensitivity analysis. Each sensitivity shows both upper and lower extremes.

Break-Even Analysis

What is Break-Even Analysis? Break-even analysis identifies the parameter value where cash flow after debt service equals zero. This is a critical threshold - values below break-even require owner contributions, while values above break-even generate positive returns.

Safety Margin: The margin shows how far the base case is from break-even. A larger margin provides more protection against adverse changes. Parameters with margins below 25% are considered higher risk.

Methodology & Assumptions

Sensitivity Analysis Methodology

This sensitivity analysis employs a comprehensive one-way and two-way parameter variation approach to assess investment risk and return variability. Each parameter is systematically varied across a realistic range while holding all other parameters constant at their base case values.

One-Way Sensitivity: Tests individual parameter impacts by varying one parameter at a time. This approach isolates the effect of each factor and helps identify which parameters have the greatest impact on investment performance.

Two-Way Sensitivity: Examines parameter interactions by varying two parameters simultaneously. This reveals how parameters interact and identifies combinations that lead to optimal or suboptimal outcomes.

Scenario Analysis: Combines multiple parameter changes to model realistic scenarios (Optimistic, Base, Pessimistic). This provides a framework for understanding the range of possible outcomes under different market conditions.

Parameter Range Justifications

  • Occupancy Rate (30-70%): Based on Engelberg market data and seasonal patterns. 30% represents low-demand scenarios, 70% represents strong demand.
  • Daily Rate (120-300 CHF): Reflects pricing strategies from discounted off-peak rates to premium peak-season rates, calibrated to local market conditions.
  • Interest Rate (1.2-3.5%): Covers current low-rate environment to historical averages, accounting for potential rate increases.
  • Management Fee (20-35%): Represents typical range for property management services in Swiss vacation rental markets.
  • Loan-to-Value (60-80%): Standard range for Swiss real estate financing, balancing leverage and equity requirements.

Key Assumptions

  • Time Horizon: 15-year investment period with property sale at end of period
  • Inflation Rate: 2% annual inflation applied to revenues and variable expenses
  • Property Appreciation: 2.5% annual appreciation rate
  • Discount Rate: 3% for NPV calculations (realistic for real estate investments)
  • Tax Considerations: Analysis is pre-tax; actual returns may vary based on individual tax situations
  • Market Stability: Assumes no major market disruptions or regulatory changes

Limitations

  • One-Way Analysis Limitation: One-way sensitivity assumes parameters are independent. In reality, parameters may be correlated (e.g., higher occupancy may require lower rates).
  • Static Assumptions: Analysis uses fixed assumptions for inflation, appreciation, and discount rates. Actual rates may vary over time.
  • No Tax Considerations: Analysis is pre-tax. Tax benefits (depreciation, deductions) and tax liabilities may significantly impact actual returns.
  • Market Risk: Analysis does not account for market crashes, regulatory changes, or other black swan events.
  • Operating Assumptions: Assumes consistent property management quality and maintenance standards. Actual operating performance may vary.
  • Financing Assumptions: Assumes fixed-rate financing. Variable-rate mortgages introduce additional interest rate risk.

Industry Benchmarks

  • Cap Rates: Swiss vacation rental properties typically show cap rates of 3-6%, depending on location and property type
  • Occupancy Rates: Engelberg vacation rentals typically achieve 40-60% annual occupancy, with strong seasonality
  • Management Fees: Standard range is 20-30% of gross rental income for full-service management
  • Cash-on-Cash Returns: Levered vacation rental investments typically target 5-10% cash-on-cash returns
  • IRR Targets: Real estate investors typically target 8-12% IRR for vacation rental properties
